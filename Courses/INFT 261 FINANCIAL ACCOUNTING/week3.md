# RECORDING BUSINESS TRANSACTIONS

## Accounting cycle
* **Accounting cirlce**: e series of business transactions occurring during the accounting period and its recording

### Phaes or an accounting circle
* **Recording of transactions**: as soon as the tranaction happens.
* **Journal**:  transactions are recorded in Journal chronologically.
* **Ledger**: all journals are classified in a ledger in a classified manner.
* **Trial balance**: balance prepared for financial statements
* **Adjusted trial balance**.
* **Closing entries**: nominal accounts are closed by transferring to either trading account, profit account or loss account.
* **Financial statements**.

### SOURCE DOCUMENTS
* These are written documents holding details of business transactions.examples include:
* **Invoice/bill**
* **Receipts**: issued when cash is received from a customer.
* **Debit note**: evidence showing that supplier account is debited. Prepared in case of purchase return. 
* **Credit note**: Evidence showing that a customer account is credited. Prepare in case goods are received from the customer.
* **Pay in slip**: used for depositing cash or cheque into bank
* **Cheque**: an order in writing drawn upon a bank to pay a specified sum to the bearer


### Voucher:
A source by which we record transactions. 3 types and they include:
1. **A Payment voucher** usually on a printed standard form, is a record of payment.
2. **A Receipt voucher** is a document which is issued against cash receipts.
3. **A Transfer voucher** is used to record the residuary transactions. An internal transaction or a transaction not involving any cash payment or cash receipt, is recorded in the transfer voucher.


### CAUSES OF CHANGE IN CAPITAL
* Additional investment
* Profits
* Drawings: money taken from the business by the owner for personal use.
* Losses

## APPROACHES TO INCOME MEASUREMENT
Net income of a business is measured using 2 approaches. 

### ASSETS AND LIABILITIES APPROACH
* **Net income** is the difference between the ending capital and the 
beginning capital of a given period, subject to adjustments for additional capital and drawings within the period.

### REVENUES AND EXPENSES APPROACH
* **Net income** is the difference between the sum of revenues 
and gains on one hand and the sum of expenses and losses on the other. 

## JOURNAL
* Historical record of business transaction or events. Diary for the business.
* **Journal Entry**.
    - Process recording business transactions in a journal is called Journalizing.

#### **Columns in a journal**
1. Date
2. Particulars: Name of accounts to be debited are written first, then name of accounts to be credited written lastly.
3. L.F Ledger folio - shows the page in the ledger in which various accounts appear.
4. Debit(Dr) amount 
5. Credit(Cr) amount

### Advantages of using a journal
* Contain permanent records of all transactions
* Easy searching of transactions since they are in logical orders
* Complete information relating to a transaction is available at one place.
* Transaction is provided with an explanation
* Reduces possible errors.
* One source of truth for debit and crdit information
* Avoids omissions or duplications of transactions
* Journal allows delayed posting into ledger.

### Steps in Creating a Journal
* Determine accounts affected by the transaction
* Determine nature of affected accounts
* Determine accounts to be debited and the accounts to be credited
* Determine the amounts of credit and debit
* Record date (date and month), year at the top
* Record particulars
* Make a brief description of the transaction in the particulars on next line.
* Draw line accross particulars to seperate one journal entry from another.
