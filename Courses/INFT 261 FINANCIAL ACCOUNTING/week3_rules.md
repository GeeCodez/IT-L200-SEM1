## RULES OF DEBIT AND CREDIT
Account name

| Left or Debit side | Right or Credit side |

*Debits are written to the left side and the credits to the right side*

### Double entry rule for Assets and Liabilities

*Increase in assets are debits(right side) and decrease in assets are credits(right side)*

*Increase in liability is a credit(right side) and decrease is debit(right side)*

### CAPITAL 
Owners resources invested in the business is called capital. Owner's account is called **capital account** 

**Capital accounts** are credits.
*Increase in capital account is credit and when capital reduces in case of losses or withdrawal for personal use, its debit*

**You have to note that any time a transaction takes place two accounts are affected. One account will be 
debited and the other account credited to complete the double entry**

*Increase liability credit, increase capital credit, increase assets debit*

### DOUBLE ENTRY FOR REVENUE AND EXPENSES
* **Revenue** is the income earned from sales of goods and services.sources: rent received, discount received, commmisions, interest.

* *Increasing revenue is credit and decrease is debit*
* **Sales**: sale of goods which were purchased with the main aimof reselling.Account is called **sales** account.
* **Goods purchased** account is called *purchases*. These are goods bought with the intention of selling them to make profit.
* **Increase in sales is credit** and **decrease is debit**.

## Expenses
* **Expenses**: costs incurred in running business on day-to-day basis. eg: wages, salaries, rent, rates, electricity.
* *Increase in expenses are debits and decrease in expenses are credits*

## THE ACCOUNTING EQUATION
* If an owner provides all the capital for the business, **ASSETS=CAPITAL**
    - The resources in the business = Resources supplied by the owner.

* When a third party is involved, **ASSETS=CAPTIAL+LIABILITIES**
    - Resources in the business = Resources supplied by the owner + resources supplied by third party.


### ASSETS AND LIABILITIES
#### ASSETS
* Resources controlled by the business entity as a result of past events for which economic benefits are expected to flow to the firm.
* **Non-current Assets**:  assets are acquired by the business to assist in earning revenues and not for resale. 
    - Land and buildings, plant and machinery, fixtures, furnitures, fittings, equipments, vehicles

* **Current Assets**: Don't last more than a year and are related to the trading activities of the firm. 
    - stock of goods, trade debtors/accounts receivable, other debtors, cash at bank, cash in hand.

#### LIABILITIES
* Obligations of a business as a result of past events settlement of which is expected to result in an economic outflow of amounts from the firm.
* **Non-current liabilities**: expected to last or paid after one year.
    - long term loans.
* **Current Liabilities**: Will be paid withing a year.
    - buying goods on credit, bank overdraft, creditors, trade payables

**Capital is the residual amount on the owner's interest in the firm after deducting liabilities from assets.

* **CURRENT ASSETS - CURRENT LIABILITIES = NET CURRENT ASSETS**
* **NET CURRENT ASSETS + NON-CURRENT ASSETS = NET ASSETS**
* **NET ASSETS = CAPITAL + NON-CURRENT ASSETS**

* *Current liabilities* are listed in order of which is most due for payment.
* *Non current assets are listed in order of permanence, whiles current assets are listed in order of liquidity*. 

* *The Accounting equation can be expressed in a simple report called the Statement of financial position*
