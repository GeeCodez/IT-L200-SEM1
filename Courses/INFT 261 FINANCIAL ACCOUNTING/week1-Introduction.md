## ACCOUNTING
* **Accounting**: is the process of recording, classifying, summarizing, analyzing, interpreting and reporting the financial transactions of a business for the interest of management and stakeholders.
* To ascertain the financial position and the profitability of the business.
* It concerns revenues, expenses, assets and liabilites of a business.
* useful tool to manage and report to external users.

**Accounting is concerned with**:
* Recording data(collecting,analyzing and recording)
* summarizing data(summaring and presenting)
* Interpreting data(communicating what has been learned from the data)

### Objectives of accounting
* **Provide information for decision making**
* For rational decision making
* Ascertain operational profit and loss
* To ascertain the financial position of the business
* To provide information to various users
* To comply with legal, statutory and contractual obligations
* For management to render reports of their stewardship to the business owners.

**Assests** are things that can bring profit and help the business to operate effectively

**Liabilities** are the financial obligations that the business is supposed to meet and they don't bring any profit to the business.

## Business Transactions
* **Business transaction** is an event which involves the transfer of money or money's worth of financial events.
* Results in change in the financial position of a business enterprise.
* All transactions are events but not all events are transactions.
* There is movement of value from one person to another.

**Source documents**: receipts, vouchers, invoices, bank statements.

### TYPES OF ACCOUNTING 
* **Financial accounting**
* **Mangement accounting**: type of accounting that provides information in a way that is good for policy making and running day-to-day operations of the business.
* **Cost accounting**: type of accounting that helps to determine cost, facilitate planning and control of the business activities and to supply information for short and long term decision making.

### FINANCIAL ACCOUTING VS MANAGEMENT ACCOUNTING
| Basis | Financial accounting | Management accounting |
| 1. Purpose of information | For reporting to external users | To provide cost information to management |
| 2. Scope | describes the whole of the business | Focuses on small parts |
| 3. Legal requirements | Mandatory for Public limited liability companies | Optionsal |
| 4. Nature of information | Mostly financial | Both finance and non-finane |
| 5. Format and regulation | Must be according to legal requirements | No required format |

### Disadvantages of Financial Accounting
* Records only quantitative information
* It provides historical information which limits predictability
* Provides whole business information
* Does not help in knowing the cost behaviour.
