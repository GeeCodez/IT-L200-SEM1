# SOCIETY & GROUP INFERENCE

## SOCIETY
People in general considered in relation to the laws,organizations that make it possible for them to live together is termed society.

### TYPES OF SOCIETY
* **Hunting and gathering**: survives on hunting
* **Pastoral**: pasture of animals for food and transport
* **Horticultural**: cultivating fruits, veges, plants
* **Agricultural society**: Technology for crop cultivation
* **Feudal**: Land ownership
* **Industrial**: Use of machines for production
* **Post-industrial**: use of computers and micro chips

### Groups 
* **A group** is a social system in which members have regular contact, act cooperatively and share common goals.
* Groups help us learn the skills, knowledge, and behaviors needed to function as consumers.
* **Primary groups**: intimate groups we come together with frequently.eg. family, church
* **Secondary groups**: less intimacy and interation. Less influence.
* **Formal groups**: organized and defined structure, hierachy based. Roles are assigned.
* **Informal groups**: emerge from formal groupsl
* **Roles**: patterns of behavior expected of an individual in a given social context.
* **Status**: relative position of a person in a group's social spectrum.

### Reference Groups
Groups that serve as a reference for an individual in forming general or specific values.
* **Normative**: influence general or broadly defined values.
* **Comparative**: benchmark specific attitudes or behaviors.
* **Indirect**: individuals or groups with whom a person does not have direct contact.
* **Membership Reference groups**: groups you already belong to and follow their behaviour. eg: Family, classmates,etc.
* **Aspirational groups**: groups you may want to belong to and you imitate their lifestyle. eg: celebrities.
* **Dissociative**: groups you don't want to associate with.

### FACTORS THAT AFFECT REFERENCE GROUP INFLUENCE
* Nature and extend of consumers experience
* Credibility, power and attractiveness of the group
* Conspicuousness of the product.

### CONSUMER RELATED REFERENCE GROUPS
1. **Friendship groups**: friends influence product a consumer selects.
2. **Shopping groups**: for social-to-share,informational and risk reduction reasons.
3. **Work groups**: work teams or friends from work can influence purchase.
4. **Consumer action groups**: provide information to consumers about specific goods.

### REFERENCE GROUP APPEALS
Reference group appeals use social groups to influence consumer choices
* **Celebrity appeals**: testimonials, endorsements, actor, spokesman.
* **Expert appeals**: because of a person's experience,he is in a position to help consumers evaluate a product.
* **The common man appeal**: slice of life commercials, satisfied customers, etc.
* **The executive spokesperson**: CEO's credibility for product evaluation.
