# CONTEXT
* **Endophoric context**
* **Exophoric context**

## Endophoric context
When a word in a sentence refers to something mentioned somewhere else in the same text.

**Things that writers use to give you clues about the context.**: 
* Restatement: explains the word again.
* Examples.
* Comparison: showing similarity. Eg; dandelion like other herbs, is good for tea.
* Contrast: showing differences. 

### **Anapho(ric/ra) reference**: 
* References backward. 
* Eg: The class is more lively because the students prepare for it.

### **Cataphoric reference**: 
* References forward
* Eg: 1. when they came here, the president and his deputy looked up
      2. this is what i want you to do, stop complaining.


## EXOPHORIC
When the meaning is not inside the text.

**You normally udnerstand them due to**:
* History
* Personal opinions
* Personal biases and predujices
* Cultural background
* World view
